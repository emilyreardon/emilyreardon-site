# Emily Reardon — Design for Futures That Love Us

## What You'll Find

I design, make, and study digital experiences and systems that help people grow, connect, and participate in the world around them. Using both hands-on materials and digital tools, I blend craft, play, storytelling, and technology to create open systems that advance agency, belonging, and care. My work spans learning, public media, and social practice art, integrating computational systems with creative practice, teaching, and community collaboration to strengthen participation and civic engagement.
I experiment with open and ethical tools, developing multimodal experiences that bring people together and imagine futures that support curiosity, connection, and shared responsibility. My practice bridges technology, learning, and civic systems, exploring how digital tools can deepen—not replace—our connection to each other and the world we inhabit.
Across solo, collaborative, and public projects, I focus on the needs, curiosity, and well-being of people, communities, and the living world around them. I aim to build environments and tools that nurture participation, expand access, and encourage agency, imagining futures that are inclusive, participatory, and generative.
